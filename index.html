The issue
Period poverty is a crisis.

In Kenya, the lack of menstrual products isn't just an inconvenience - it changes the entire course of a girl's life. Here's what's really happening.

4 days
Many girls are forced to miss up to 4 days of school every month because they have no hygiene products or access to washrooms. Over a year, that's weeks of lost education.
Unsafe alternatives
Girls in slum communities are often forced to use rags, leaves, or torn mattress stuffing instead of pads - leading to UTIs, reproductive infections, and long-term health consequences.
High-risk situations
When products cost too much, some girls are driven into transactional and exploitative situations just to afford basic hygiene. This is a safety crisis, not just a health one.
"Menstrual hygiene is more than pads - it's health, dignity, and rights! When girls can manage their periods safely and confidently, they stay in school, thrive, and own their future." - Global South Coalition for Dignified Menstruation

What we do
How we make a difference

We are a group of high school students in Portsmouth, Virginia who believe that where you're born shouldn't determine whether you have access to basic hygiene. So we're doing something about it.

Collect & fundraise
We run donation drives and fundraising events across Portsmouth to gather hygiene products and funds.
Send to Kenya
Everything collected goes directly to girls in disadvantaged communities in Kenya through Hearts4Hygiene's network.
Raise awareness
We speak up about period poverty so that more people understand why this work matters and join the cause.
